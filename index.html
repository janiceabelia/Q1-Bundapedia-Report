📊 Executive Marketing Summary

Q1 defined a massive shift from passive presence to aggressive acquisition. In January (Weeks 1-4), the account operated on a strict organic baseline, struggling to surpass 2,000 views per week. Following the strategic intervention in February and March, aggressive Paid Ads were introduced, driving volume exponentially ("meledak").

The March Week 4 Organic Truth: March Weeks 10-11 saw explosive, unprecedented traffic purely driven by ad-spend scaling (peaking at 2.2 Million views). However, the most critical data point is March Week 12 (W4). When ad spend was paused and content reverted to purely organic, the account generated 3,354 organic views. While this looks like a drop compared to the 2.2M ad spike, it actually represents a +76% increase in our underlying organic baseline compared to January Week 4 (1,903 views). Our paid media successfully built a stickier organic audience.

🚀 Q2 Advanced Marketing Strategies

1. The "Post-Ad" Retention Protocol

March W4 (W12) proved that turning off ads drops views significantly, but our floor (3.3k views) is now much higher than January.

  • Action: Implement TikTok Retargeting Audiences. Create a Custom Audience of users who watched >50% of the viral 2.2M March Ad videos.
  • Goal: Serve them lower-cost, highly organic-feeling "community" videos in Q2 to convert casual ad-viewers into permanent followers.

2. Fixing the Share-to-Like Ratio (Virality Metric)

Out of 23k total engagements, only 1,163 were shares. The TikTok algorithm prioritizes Shares and Saves for organic virality.

  • Action: Pivot Q2 organic content frameworks to be "Utility & Relatability" heavy. Use hooks like "Bunda, save this recipe for later..." or "Send this to a new mom who needs to hear this."
  • Goal: Increase share velocity to decrease our reliance on Paid Ads for reach.

3. Hook Fatigue & Creative Refresh

The aggressive scaling in Feb W8 (1.6M views) and Mar W11 (2.2M views) will eventually lead to Ad Fatigue. Our CPA will rise if we recycle the same creatives.

  • Action: Run a micro A/B test (using the AB Testing tracker) strictly on the first 3 seconds of our best Q1 videos. Change ONLY the text hook or the opening visual.
  • Goal: Recycle winning Q1 concepts without triggering algorithm ad-fatigue.

4. Blended Content Strategy (The 80/20 Rule)

Now that we have established a baseline, Q2 must balance the ad budget responsibly.

  • Action: 80% of budget goes to amplifying proven organic winners (Wait 24 hours after posting. If organic traction is >3k views naturally, boost it). 20% goes to pure direct-response ads.
  • Goal: Lower overall marketing spend while maintaining the momentum gained in March.
